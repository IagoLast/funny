The idea

In the design area any source can be useful to create something new. Sometimes, we are too focused on getting inspiration from things on the internet, other designerâ€™s projects or by a short brainstorming. Each artist has its own creative process but looking for inspiration out of the comfort zone can help in some cases. I started this project or visual experiment with the purpose of creating a colorful collection of photographs to take chromatic palettes from.

The solution

During a short trip around Portugal I took photographs of every colorful spot I found from north to south. After putting all the images together the result looked closer to a chromatic representation of a country. For each photograph I extracted a color palette with what I think were the predominant four tones along with their Pantone coding. The design of the book itself is based on a simple grid composed by photographs. It includes a short introduction of the project in the beginning. The general white aspect tries to highlight the colors. The book is made in two different paper types, coptic biding and a hardcover with a window in the middle.
